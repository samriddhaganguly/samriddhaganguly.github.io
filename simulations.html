Relativistic Dirac Dispersion
Theoretical Background: Dirac Dispersion

The relativistic Dirac Hamiltonian describes low-energy quasiparticles in topological materials, graphene, and surface states of topological insulators. The energy spectrum forms a characteristic double cone (Dirac cone) at zero mass. A finite mass gap m opens when time-reversal or inversion symmetry is broken — driving topological phase transitions.

E(k) = ±√(v_f²k² + m²)

Kitaev Chain: Majorana Zero Modes
PHASE: TOPOLOGICAL
Theoretical Background: Majorana Zero Modes in 1D

In the Kitaev chain model of a 1D p-wave superconductor, Majorana fermions appear as zero-energy excitations localized at the ends when |μ| < 2|t|. The topological phase transition occurs at the critical lines μ = ±2t. These modes are protected by the bulk gap and are candidates for fault-tolerant quantum computing.

E = ±√((ε−μ)² + Δ²)

Bulk–Boundary Correspondence
Theoretical Background: Bulk–Boundary Correspondence

In Chern insulators, the quantized Hall conductance is determined by the bulk Chern number C. This topology forces gapless chiral states at the physical edges — modes that propagate in a single direction and are protected from backscattering by the bulk gap. The number of chiral edge modes equals |C|.

N_edge = |C_bulk|

Theoretical Background: The Bloch Sphere

The Bloch sphere is the geometric representation of the pure state space of a two-level quantum system (qubit). The north and south poles represent |0⟩ and |1⟩. Any point on the surface represents a coherent superposition. Quantum gates are rotations on this sphere.

|ψ⟩ = cos(θ/2)|0⟩ + e^(iφ)sin(θ/2)|1⟩
